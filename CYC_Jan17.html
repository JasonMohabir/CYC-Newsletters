Disaster Speakers January 29th

Disaster Speaks is a powerful school-based event that gives you the opportunity to personalize disaster. Large boxes will represent homes lost and will have stories attached about someone who has made the unthinkable journey through disaster. After viewing the boxes, students will have the opportunity to share the steps they will take to become prepared and fill out an American Red Cross Emergency Communication Card

National Blood Donor Month!

The Red Cross has determined January National Blood Donor Month, to help keep the blood supply high, and to save as many lives as possible. Every two seconds, someone in the US needs blood. During the winter, there is generally a dip in Red Cross blood donations due to the cold, during the season when blood is needed the most. Find a local blood drive here! Healthy individuals over 17 years old and over 110 pounds are generally eligible to donate. Blood can be donated every 56 days. Even if you can’t donate blood, you can get involved by organizing a blood drive at your school or in your community! For more information on this process, please visit here, or contact Susan Sommer at susan.sommer@redcross.org.

Youth Preparedness Week January 16th - 20th

The Red Cross Youth Preparedness week will take place from January 16th through January 20th! Opportunities to get involved with disaster preparedness, and to prepare and protect your own family from disaster, include:

  • Gather your family to create an Emergency Plan, using the Red Cross templates here. A few minutes of planning can keep you ready for any disaster.
  • Encourage your younger siblings and relatives to download the Monster Guard: Prepare for Emergencies App, designed for 7-11 year olds to understand how to prepare for various emergencies, including weather and home fires.
  • Get them to read the “Mickey and Friends Disaster Activity Book”, designed by Disney, in English and Spanish.
