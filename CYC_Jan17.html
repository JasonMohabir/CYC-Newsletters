Disaster Speakers January 29th

Disaster Speaks is a powerful school-based event that gives you the opportunity to personalize disaster. Large boxes will represent homes lost and will have stories attached about someone who has made the unthinkable journey through disaster. After viewing the boxes, students will have the opportunity to share the steps they will take to become prepared and fill out an American Red Cross Emergency Communication Card

National Blood Donor Month!

The Red Cross has determined January National Blood Donor Month, to help keep the blood supply high, and to save as many lives as possible. Every two seconds, someone in the US needs blood.

  • During the winter, there is generally a dip in Red Cross blood donations due to the cold, during the season when blood is needed the most.
  • Find a local blood drive here! Healthy individuals over 17 years old and over 110 pounds are generally eligible to donate. Blood can be donated every 56 days. Even if you can’t donate blood, you can get involved by organizing a blood drive at your school or in your community!
  • For more information on this process, please visit here, or contact Susan Sommer at susan.sommer@redcross.org.

Youth Preparedness Week January 16th - 20th

The Red Cross Youth Preparedness week will take place from January 16th through January 20th! Opportunities to get involved with disaster preparedness, and to prepare and protect your own family from disaster, include:

  • Gather your family to create an Emergency Plan , using the Red Cross templates here. A few minutes of planning can keep you ready for any disaster.
  • Encourage your younger siblings and relatives to download the Monster Guard: Prepare for Emergencies App , designed for 7-11 year olds to understand how to prepare for various emergencies, including weather and home fires.
  • Get them to read the “Mickey and Friends Disaster Activity Book”, designed by Disney, in English and Spanish.
  • Explore other Red Cross apps here!

Missing Maps February 22nd

Join us on Wednesday, February 22nd for the Missing Maps Project hosted by the American Red Cross Greater New York Region! This is an exciting opportunity contribute to an international humanitarian project while networking with your fellow students across the Greater New York Region.
What is Missing Maps? Missing Maps is a humanitarian project that preemptively maps parts of the world that are vulnerable to natural disasters, conflicts, and disease epidemics. The project was founded in 2014 and has so far mapped large towns and cities in countries such as South Sudan, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and the Central African Republic.

Volunteer Intake Processing

Looking for a new and exciting volunteer opportunity? Perhaps you would be interested in becoming a Volunteer Intake Processing (VIP) Center volunteer! Our national VIP Center is currently staffed by existing volunteers and we are looking for additional youth to join the team! You can work from home or from your local Red Cross office.

What are the requirements for this role?

  • Comfortable speaking with prospective volunteers over the phone
  • Comfortable working with computers
  • Being able to dedicate two to four hours a week on volunteer intake
